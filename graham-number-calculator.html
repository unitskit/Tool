About Graham Number

The Graham Number is a measure of a stock's fundamental value, calculated as:

Graham Number = √(22.5 × EPS × Book Value)

This formula suggests the maximum price an investor should pay for a stock. The number 22.5 comes from Graham's recommendation of paying no more than 15 times earnings and 1.5 times book value (15 × 1.5 = 22.5).
